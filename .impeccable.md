## Design Context

### Users
Nature enthusiasts, students, and conservationists who want to identify species and understand their ecological impact. They value accuracy, speed, and a clean, non-distracting interface.

### Brand Personality
**Clinical, Warm, Authoritative.**
Like a modern museum exhibit or a high-end botanical field guide. It should feel like a tool for discovery, not just a tech demo.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light-first with a rich, deep-green dark mode.
- **Visual Style**: "Botanical Precision". Solid surfaces, meticulous borders, and generous white space. No glassmorphism or generic gradients.
- **Palette (OKLCH)**:
  - Primary: `oklch(0.55 0.15 145)` (Lush Green)
  - Secondary: `oklch(0.25 0.05 150)` (Deep Forest)
  - Background: `oklch(0.99 0.01 120)` (Warm Paper)
- **Typography**:
  - Headings: **`Zilla Slab`**.
  - Body: **`Figtree`**.

### Design Principles
1. **Specimen First**: Every identified species should feel like a valuable entry in a field guide.
2. **Intentional White Space**: Use space to create focus and calm, avoiding the "empty" feeling by using purposeful margins and centered compositions.
3. **Meticulous Detailing**: Fine borders (0.5px - 1px), subtle shadows, and crisp typography over decorative blurs.
4. **Frictionless Discovery**: The path from opening the app to seeing an identification should be as short as possible, with the API key setup feeling like a "Level Up" rather than a barrier.
